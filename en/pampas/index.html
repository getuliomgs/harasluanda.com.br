The Pampa coat is truly the Brazilian denomination for the USA Tobiano horses, but in Brazil the passionate Brazilian breeders decided to create an association called ABCPAMPA – Brazilian Pampa Breeders Association.




by Lúcio Sérgio Andrade

The Pampa Horse conquered international fame for being the bad guy’s horses in the western cinema and Indians and army matches. Ridden by the Indians without saddles, generally with colorful skins, originals mustangs from American prairies, today they charm millions of people in the whole world for its skin beauty or velocity, agility, structure and courage.

As a breed, the Pampa is already world wide known as the “Paint Horse”, The American Quarter Horse, or for the Spanish name “Pinto”. The most important difference between the Paint and the Pinto Horses is that the last one doesn’t show the work morphology, that featuring the American Quarter. The beginning of American Pampa is by the year of 1519, when the Spanish Explorer Hermano Cortês brought up to American Continent a troop of 16 war horses and among them, one was white with dark spots on its belly. From the genetical crossing between this horse and native American mustangs, raised the ‘Pinto’ and ‘Paint’ Horses.

Populated by herds of wild horses the American West was tamed on the legs of Pampas, with their great skin, they became the Indians favorites, especially by the Comanche, well known for their great skills as western horses raiders; they could be faster then the chivalry, approaches and trains. The Comanche idolized the Pampa horses as the God’s chosen, believing they were the God’s favorites.

In Brazil there’s no precisely registration for the first Pampas, but it’s believed that this coat was introduced here by some few Barb breed horses, brought by Portuguese settlers and especially by the Dutch horses, introduced in the territory during Dutch invasion in the state of Pernambuco. By these breeds is also introduced in Brazil a kind of natural trot gait, reason for which the Brazilian Pampa shows, beyond their beautiful kinds of skins, another relevant market factor: the gait. This functional featuring qualifies the national Pampa as an ideal horse to leisure – to be used on ridings, runs, tourism, competitions. On international market a gaited Pampa is a rare like a jewel that you can not set a price for.

The origem for the name Pampa is this: by the middle of XIX century, o Brigadier Rafael Tobias Aguiar, defeated on the Sorocaba revolution, run away with his army to the state of Rio Grande do Sul, there He faced the Farrapos battle. Most of the soldiers ridden a Tobiano, as the Pampas were earlier known in the south of the country. When he came back to São Paulo, these horses became known gradually in the rest of the country as Pampa horses (which was the codename on Rio Grande do Sul battle).

On the opposite of APHA - American Paint Horse Association – objectives, The ABCPAMPA – Brazilian Pampa Breeders Association don’t register quarter horses descendents and basically all exotics breeds, to don’t change the morphological breed features o four national Pampa horses. The Brazilian Pampa is based on the international saddle pattern, keeping all the types of gaits that show the real meaning of a multifunctional horse.
