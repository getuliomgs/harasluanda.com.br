An old passion that renews in every new generation

Born in July 10th, 1836, by 1870 Cassiano Campolina, won the pregnant mare Medeia from which was born a foal called Monarca, that the father was one of Emperor D. Pedro II’s horses, an Andalusia breed horse. The born of the foal Monarca starts a story of conquest and success: the story of Campolina breed.

Cassiano’s goal was to create large horses, which qualities were agility, endurance and good appearance. For that, he selected and crossed breeds, such as Marchador and Thoroughbred, using his intuition and experience.

In 1904, after more than 30 years of hard work on his propose, Cassiano Campolina passed away. However, his and his friends’ dedication, the breed He created became great and better. The families of Joaquim Pacheco de Resende and Coronel Gabriel Gonçalves were very important in this mission. By the years other great breeders showed up as Agenor Sampaio, Alfredo Manuel Fernandes, Américo de Oliveira, Américo Ferreira Leite, Antonio Lopes da Silva, Arnaldo Bezerra, Cel. Linto Diniz, Ascanio Diniz, Emir Cadar, Epaminondas Cunha Melo, Fernando Diniz Oliveira, Geraldo Magela Resende, Guaracy Engel Vieira, Guido Pacheco Magalhães, Heitor Lambertucci, Jamil Saliba, José Eugenio Câmara Dutra, José Ferreira Leite, José Geraldo Areias, Leonardo Campos, Luiz Eduardo Cortez (DEADO), Lídio Araujo, Orminio de Almeida, Pedro Joaquim Carlos, Roberto Catelmo, Severino Veloso, Tonico Figueiredo, Valdemar Resende Urbano, Valério Resende, and more.

70 years later, the breed was developed without a default, by the breeders references and because of that it was necessary to set a standart for the breed in the way all breeders got together to develop the breed technical features and qualities. Brazilian Association of Campolina Breeders (Associação Brasileira dos Criadores do Cavalo Campolina) was then founded in 1951, in Belo Horizonte. Nowadays the breeders are responsible to continue this story that consolidates the breed every year, gained more admirers.
