The rise of a passion

When I was a boy, my father owned a grange in a region called Recôncavo Baiano, more necessarily about 25Km far from the cities of Cachoeira e Santo Amaro da Purificação (land of Caetano Veloso and Maria Betânia). There were 02 crossbred horses: a tordilha mare and a sorrel horse called “Boy”, that was very good on trot, and therefore I loved to spend the day riding it.
Then someday, I remember well, my father asked me:
- You like horses a lot, don’t you Paulo?
Without any doubt, I answered:
-I Love it! And someday I’ll have a haras.


The rise of an impassioned

Many difficulties came up to my life. My parents got divorced when I was 10 years old. I had to work hard since the first year at medical school. I worked hard as a book seller, I sold ozone water filters and finally I worked with importation of products from Paraguay. That was the only way to conciliate work and studies, as in the sellers area I could flexibly my time, using the weekends for the shopping trips and the free time between the classes to sell the products. By this routine, I could graduate on regular period, without fail in any subjects. In the beginning I worked in the cities of Dias D'Ávila and Camaçari, in the metropolitan area of Salvador. In 1992, with 20 colleagues, I founded a cooperative that became the greatest one of North and northeast and one of the greatest in Brazil.


The birth of a dream

In August 2003, on the Exporural, walking on city’s Exhibition Center, in the company of my older daughter (Thalita), I got charmed by a Pampa coat Campolina that was being ridden by a friend (then I discovered that the animal was called Jupiter do Porto Rico). I asked if the horse was for sale and the friend replied:
- The owner won’t sell this one, but the foal that is its descendant will be auctioned on Thursday’s auction.

Even without own any farm to house the animal, I attended to my daughter’s request and I went to the auction to buy one of those horses. Without any friendly face, as I didn’t know anyone who was a Campolina breeder, I couldn’t buy Jupiter’s foal as it was too expensive. I thought I would not buy anything, when it entered the last batch: it was the mare Pattern plating Graña, a Pampa sorrel mare that I could not resist and I bought it.

Then I had to find a place to house the mare. Initially I rent a bay. Later, I bought a farm in Araçás, 95 km from Salvador. The place is still the same one where Haras Luanda is located.


When the boy’s wish became true

By Fenagro, 2003, I’ve already owned the farm when a friend known as “Emanuel do Rato” introduced me to Claudemiro Santana, who was a candidate for the presidencial election of Associação Baiana dos Criadores de Cavalo Campolina (Bahia Association of Campolina Horses Breeders). By this time I was invited by Miro to be part of it´s executive board of directors. I had some doubts as I was starting on the Campolina business, I didn’t have much experience, but He convinced me:
- You were born a Director, so you can’t refuse the invitation!

I did not believe that the invitation was real, so I didn’t attend to the first meetings. Then the secretary called me, demanding my presence and I decided to try it. Thereafter it was a growing passion that took over my life, both for the wonderful breed, and for the nice people who were involved that eventually was formed a real family.

And the thing got really right. The name for the Haras was a tribute to my wife, who was born in Luanda, Angola, and came to Brazil in 1975, bolted the Civil War. Then, in 2005, I went to visit the National Exhibition of the Paso Fino breed, in the United States, with Lucio Andrade. I was very impressed with what I saw and I realized I could apply some of those new practices in our ABCCC (Brazilian Association of Campolina Horse Breeders). A great example of a new practice that could be applied here was an auction of ova and semen, donated by Campolina breeders, to create a fund for implementing the breed Marketing - an idea that worked so well that resulted in a profit of more than 200 thousand Dollars.

By this point, I was completely involved as a Campolina breeder. Every time I learned a bit more, I was ready to take audacious steps in invest on horses with a great genetics. Today I can say that be a horse breeder is one of the greatest motivation and pleasure in my life.

Paulo Rocha – Haras Luanda owner.
